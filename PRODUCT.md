# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Women aged 18–45 living in the GCC region (primarily UAE), bilingual in Arabic and English. They deal with acne, oily skin, pigmentation, sun damage, or a damaged skin barrier in a hot, humid, high-UV climate. Context of use: in private moments before or after their skincare ritual, often at a mirror, sometimes mid-product-shopping. Job to be done: understand what is actually going on with their skin, get a routine they trust, and check whether a product they are about to buy is halal-friendly and not full of irritants. They want a knowledgeable second opinion they can carry in their pocket, in their language, calibrated for their climate.

## Product Purpose

Nourah is an Arabic-first, halal-ingredient-aware AI skincare app calibrated for GCC skin concerns (melasma, extreme UV, high humidity). It exists because no current app combines those three properties. It does three things: AI face scan with skin scoring, barcode-based ingredient analysis with halal flagging, and personalized routine recommendations. Success looks like a user opening Nourah weekly, trusting the result, and treating it as the first place she checks before buying a product. Dermatologist-backed (the founder's mother is a licensed dermatologist in Dubai), which is the credibility anchor everything else hangs on.

## Brand Personality

Warm, confident, modern. Voice is the knowledgeable friend who happens to be a dermatologist's daughter, not the clinic and not the influencer. Reassuring before prescriptive: at moments of judgment (a scan result, a flagged ingredient), the user should feel held before she feels instructed. The reference lane is Flo crossed with the Glossier app: soft, friendly, beauty-adjacent, mass-market accessible feminine, never sterile, never loud.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS dashboard.** Flat blue-and-gray admin aesthetics, dense KPI cards, hero-metric templates, side-stripe alert borders. This is a beauty and wellness app, not an analytics tool.
- **Clinical or pharmacy aesthetic.** Sterile white-and-teal medical-app look, stock medical iconography, warning-heavy red-flag visuals. Credibility comes from quiet expertise, not from looking like a hospital app.
- **Loud commerce density.** Sephora-style grids of product tiles fighting for attention, bold sale stickers, neon promotional banners. Commerce in Nourah should feel like a recommendation from someone who knows your skin, not a marketplace.
- **Generic Gen-Z neon or filtered-influencer beauty.** Hot pink on black, gradient text, glassy maximalism. Wrong audience, wrong tone.

## Design Principles

1. **Reassure first, prescribe second.** Every result moment leads with calm framing before the action list. Scores are explained before they are ranked. Bad news is contextualized, never barked.
2. **Bilingual-native, not translated.** Arabic and English are equal first-class citizens. Layouts work with Tajawal as well as DM Sans. RTL is not an afterthought, line heights and type scales are tuned for both. The user picks her language on screen one and the app does not assume.
3. **Climate-aware authenticity.** GCC reality (UV intensity, humidity, melasma risk) shapes the recommendations and the visual language. The app feels designed for Dubai, not localized into Dubai.
4. **Quiet expertise.** Dermatologist credibility shows through restraint and accuracy, not clinical sterility or jargon. Confidence without volume.
5. **Premium without pharmacy.** It belongs next to a skincare brand on the home screen, not next to a hospital app. Soft surfaces, generous spacing, considered typography, never icy.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **WCAG 2.1 AA** target across both English and Arabic surfaces.
- Bilingual parity is part of accessibility here: Arabic users get the same hierarchy, the same affordances, the same touch targets, with RTL respected from layout up.
- Color is never the sole carrier of meaning (score colors, halal status, flags) — pair with iconography and labels.
- Touch targets minimum 44pt. Body text 15pt minimum. Score numbers and skin-state language remain legible with system text scaling.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for the scan ring pulse and screen transitions.
